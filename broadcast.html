As the internet becomes a bigger part of our lives, Ransomware, a crime in which a hacker prevents access to a system until the system’s owner pays ransom to the hacker, is rapidly increasing in prominence and scope. According to the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center’s 2021 annual report, there has been a 66% increase in the number of victims of ransomware since 2020, and a 60% increase in money lost to the crime in the same timeframe.

Nirav Patel is the owner of an Internet Security Firm. He has worked to combat Ransomware in the past. One of the ways he discussed fighting ransomware is through preventative action. “Oftentimes, after a hacker finds a vulnerability they'll spend many months setting up their attack. The crime can be stopped altogether if that vulnerability can be found and closed before the hacker has the chance to attack.” ###
